You’ve got the flow exactly right! It’s much easier to understand when you look at it through the lens of your specific **Portfolio → Salesforce** integration.

In your scenario, here is how the OAuth 2.0 roles are assigned:

### The OAuth 2.0 Cast of Characters

| Role                     | Entity in your Example | Description                                                                                                                                  |
| :----------------------- | :--------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Resource Owner**       | **You (The User)**     | You are the one who owns the data (the Accounts/Leads) inside Salesforce and has the power to grant access.                                  |
| **Client**               | **Portfolio.com**      | The "Web Service" trying to get the data. It is the application that needs the token to act on your behalf.                                  |
| **Resource Server**      | **Salesforce API**     | The server holding the "goods" (Accounts, Contacts, etc.) that will only give them up if a valid token is shown.                             |
| **Authorization Server** | **Salesforce Login**   | The engine that handles your username/password, asks "Do you want to allow Portfolio.com to access your data?", and issues the codes/tokens. |

---

### Step-by-Step: The "Authorization Code" Flow

Since you mentioned a **Button**, **Credentials**, and an **Authorization Code**, you are using what's known as the **Authorization Code Grant Type**. This is the gold standard for security.

1.  **The Request:** You click "Authorize" on **Portfolio.com**. Portfolio redirects your browser to Salesforce.
2.  **The Handshake:** Salesforce asks for your login. Note that you are giving your password to **Salesforce**, not to Portfolio.com. This is the "Secret Sauce" of OAuth—Portfolio never sees your password.
3.  **The Grant:** Once you log in and click "Allow," Salesforce sends your browser back to Portfolio.com with a short-lived **Authorization Code** in the URL.
4.  **The Exchange:** Portfolio.com takes that code and its **Client Secret** (from the Connected App) and talks to Salesforce "behind the scenes" (Server-to-Server).
5.  **The Token:** Salesforce validates the secret and the code, then hands back an **Access Token**.

---

### Why "Connected App" is the MVP

In your Salesforce setup, the **Connected App** acts as the "Identity Card" for your portfolio.

- **Client ID:** Tells Salesforce _who_ is asking (Portfolio.com).
- **Client Secret:** Proves it is actually Portfolio.com and not an impostor.
- **Scopes:** Defines _what_ Portfolio is allowed to do (e.g., `api`, `web`, `refresh_token`).

### To answer your question: "Which is OAuth working?"

The "OAuth working" part is the **entire dance** of exchanging that temporary code for a token so that Portfolio.com can eventually call a Salesforce REST API like:
`GET /services/data/v60.0/sobjects/Account/`

Without OAuth, you'd have to hardcode your Salesforce password into your Portfolio code—which would be a massive security nightmare!
